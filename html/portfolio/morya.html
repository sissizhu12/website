              Morya Jewelry

              Morya Jewelry

              introduction

              Morya Jewelry prides itself on sustainable and futuristic accessories for everyone.

              In 2 months, I conducted product research and generated 13 product designs.

              For Morya's initial brand launch, I finalized 3 designs, with an expected revenue of $12k.

              my role
              product design, product research

              collaborators
              2 co-founders, 1 creative director

              tools
              Google Analytics, Miro, Canva, Autodesk Sketchbook


              the challenge

              Do consumers have preferences in line with Morya's vision?

              This was a burning question I had from the start. To better understand the target demographic, I conducted product research with online secondary data and consumer interviews.

              This analysis later supported design decisions for better usability, accessibility, and market fit.

              product sketches


              My deliverables were product designs. To ensure productive conversations with a 3D modeller and prototype manufacturers, I included material descriptions and measurements.

              how did I get started?
              (product research)


              To investigate consumer interest in sustainability, I looked at Google search queries. I noticed an increasing interest in sustainability based on total US and Canadian searches.

              Many of these searches were contextualized with looking up fashion or clothing brands, which shows there are sustainable fashion buyers for Morya to target. Rising competitors in sustainable fashion like Reformation, Mejuri, and Patagonia also reflect this demand.

              pinterest boards as collective data


              To understand one of Morya's demographics, young women, I conducted consumer analysis Pinterest, which is dominated by women 18-24 (Stacy Jo Dixon, 2024).

              Looking at the ten most popular "jewelry" inspiration boards, I noticed that saved jewelry was often metal, intricate, and had naturalistic stones.

              This suggests Morya's vision of crystals, metals, and intricate jewelry aligns with this demographic.

              reddit forums as qualitive data


              Due to Reddit's demographic skewing male and 18-29 years of age, I also reviewed forums on men's fashion and jewelry.

              This gave me valuable insights on jewelry preferences and pain points for young men.

              curating informed user personas


              In addition to my Pinterest and Reddit research, I interviewed female and male friends to ask about their experiences purchasing and wearing jewelry. I then synergized my findings by creating two user personas.

              The personas helped me organize ideas of important product factors and who I was designing for. This included jewelry wearers with budget constraints, aesthetic preferences, motor accessibility needs and sensitive skin.

              reflection


              Crafting Morya's jewelry designs allowed me to use product research to help solve a complex and physical design problem.



              Outside of consumer research, I referenced other jewelry and physical products that move with its user. I learned so much about crafting processes and the jewelry industry. Although the initiative didn't move forward due to Morya ceasing operations, I would've loved to iterate further over prototypes based on my initial product designs.
