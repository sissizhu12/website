              Utradea

              Utradea

              introduction

              Utradea is an investing news website and mobile app with stock analytics tools.

              Founded in spring of 2020, I joined the team two years later to extend and redesign parts of their user interface.

              my role
              UI/UX design, UX research

              collaborators
              1 UI/UX designer, 1 UX researcher

              tools
              Figma, LogRocket, Autodesk Sketchbook


              the challenge

              When a pricing page is visually disorganized and hard to skim, it lowers sales.

              Looking at the Logrocket statistics and heatmap for user mouse movement, I noticed that users spent too little time on the page to engage with important pricing details.

              And the areas they did engage with were not key information: large text and visual elements. Important areas that were text heavy, less visually salient, and lower on the page were less engaged with. This meant much less clicks for those areas too, including purchase buttons, which directly translates to lower sales.

              redesign result

              how did I get started?
              (reading user research)


              Due to lack of resources, Utradea didn't conduct A/B testing on the pricing page.

              To understand how to better showcase tiers of memberships plans, I referenced the next best thing: existing user research on pricing and product pages.

              Credible articles from the Nielson Nelson Group and Vitaly Friedman helped me understand optimal and common UX patterns.

              exploring layout options


              Leaning into common UX patterns, I created iterative sketches to explore how to best show the unique benefits of each membership tier.

              After a discussion with my design lead and receiving feedback, I decided to move forward with version C due to least visual clutter.

              The design goal was to highlight text information, not distract with icons.

              making a noticeable button


              I also mocked up buttons and toggles for navigation between monthly and annual prices. Comparing them next to each other, my design team and I decided to move forward with the third option.

              Our goal was to ensure the toggle button was noticeable enough to click on, which is helped by the coloured outline and centered position on the page.

              trying graphic details


              To highlight the attractiveness of the highest tier membership and its limited free trial, I created icons and banners.

              They didnâ€™t make it onto the final pricing plans page due to distracting from the purchase buttons, but it was helpful to visually test them out.

              reflection

              Although my role was UX design, I felt that my experience reflected a need for more active user research to support design solutions.


              To advocate for more internal research, I created a preliminary survey to gather quantitative and qualitative data on Utradea users' interface experience and pain points. This would ideally help guide Utradea's future website and app design decisions.

              With the support of organization stakeholders, the survey was released to the public and used to collect insights.
