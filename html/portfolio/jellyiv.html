              Jelly IV

              Jelly IV

              introduction

              Jelly IV is a health supplement brand and client I worked with at Jenn Studio.

              In 2 months, I improved their existing ecommerce website for engagement.

              The result: 12% increase in overall time on site, 38% increase in time on the homepage, 2.7% increase in returning users, and more.

              my role
              UX research, design strategy

              tools
              Figma, Google Analytics, Crazy Egg

              collaborators
              1 UX designer, 1 design manager


              the problem


              • Low sales despite growing social media and brand awareness
              • Website is the only platform used for selling product, especially mobile

              • Next steps are to investigate key website metrics, including:
                • Engagement
                • Conversion rate
                • Cart and webpage abandonment

              website initial state


              • Using Google Analytics, I noticed:

                • Jelly IV’s conversion rate was 0.55%
                • Which is lower than the industry average of 2.5%

                • But their cart abandonment rate is 44%
                • Which outperforms the industry average of 70.19%

              • Why might this be happening?
              • For a deeper dive on user behavior, I used Crazy Egg’s heatmaps and noticed:

                • Most users scroll to just after the mobile fold before dropping off

                • They are not seeing important product information near the bottom
                • Results in missed opportunities to engage users

              • How does this compare to Jelly IV’s competitors?

              competitor analysis


              To understand the CPG industry standards, I compared Jelly IV’s website with websites from other hangover supplements and indie CPGs.

              In particular, I considered Dayguard and Graza, two successful CPG brands.

              Out of all direct competititors, Dayguard:

              • Holds the highest percentage of mobile website users
              • Has branding that attracts younger consumers the most
              • Has the most engaging website


              Similarly, Graza's site is high performing and optimized for ecommerce sales. Other successful CPGs use website UX patterns similar to Graza.

              3 analysis takeaways


              1. Like competitors, Jelly IV’s users are mostly mobile
                • With limited bandwidth, they should optimize the mobile website first over desktop

              2. Jelly IV’s new website design should reflect its fun branding to attract its target consumers
                • This will help them stand out among CPGs

              3. Jelly IV should adopt similar website patterns as high performing competitors’ websites
                • This will improve user navigation and engagement on the overall site

              I then sketched wireframes based on common UX patterns and the takeaways, with the goal of improving site engagement.

              These were handed off to the UX designer to mockup a new site for Jelly IV.

              design change #1


              Due to new budget constraints, Jelly IV requested that we immediately implement only the highest impact design changes.

              The first design change I recommended was to add a “Shop Now” button in the navigation bar.

              This would drive traffic and easy access to the main product page on all site pages.

              design change #2


              Lastly, I recommended adding a featured press section in a high visibility area on the homepage: right under the hero section.

              As an emerging ecommerce brand selling products, this would lend them credibility for the product brand overall.

              design change #3


              I then sketched wireframes based on common UX patterns and the takeaways.

              These were handed off to the UX designer to mockup a new site for Jelly IV.

              design impact on engagement


              After Jelly IV implemented the design changes, Google Analytics indicated 3 key improvements:


              • 12% increased overall time on site per user
                • Previously 33 seconds
                • Currently 37 seconds

              • 38% increased time on home page per user
                • Previously 16 seconds
                • Currently 22 seconds

              • 11% increase in page views per user and overall user navigation
                • Suggests users are navigating and viewing site pages more before leaving the site

              reflection


              One key takeaway is to use competitor analysis and publicly available UX research to guide design decisions. From industry analysis, adopting common or high performing website patterns can improve site navigation.


              For Jelly IV, future next steps to optimize the website further would be to A/B test and collect data on specific branding, messaging, and visual direction on each page.
